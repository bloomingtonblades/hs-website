Dropping the Puck on Cancer

Join us for the first ever High School version of Collegiate Charities Dropping the Puck on Cancer series. Tickets are on sale now!

Also don’t miss out on your only chance to get an exclusive, made-for-this-game custom Blades hockey jersey! Order online. These will make great holiday gifts!

Stick around after the game for a Fan Fest outside the Coliseum, followed by the Indy Fuel home opener against the Tulsa Oilers! One ticket purchased gives you access to both events!

The Bloomington Blades have a long history of cooperation with Collegiate Charities. Proceeds from tickets purchased will also benefit Riley Children’s Hospital, the Carmel Icehounds hockey program, and the Collegiate Charities National Drop the Puck Foundation.

Updates

Any player who will be missing an on- or off-ice practice should let Coach Appel know in advance. Practice plans may change based on number of players attending. Thank you!

Be sure to check the calendar for updated practices times and other changes. Also players and parents should get the TeamApp mobile app. Once you install it, create an account and then search for “Blades HS Hockey” and join! We’ll use TeamApp for quick messages and game-day updates.

Reminder, players are not permitted to drive themselves to away games.

Off-Ice Practice Carpools

Sign up to carpool and share the driving for off-ice practices! These are mostly going to be at Creekbend Vineyard, 7508 N Woodall Rd, Ellettsville . Players with a driver’s license are permitted to drive to practices in the Bloomington area.

Did you know…

  • Ice rentals are $200-$250 per hour or more. Seasonal expenses for ice are tens of thousands of dollars.
  • We must pay for officials, league fees, tournament fees, coaching education, uniforms and equipment.
  • We receive NO funding from city, county, or school system sources.
  • Our coaches and board members are all volunteers.
  • Player fees alone do not cover our expenses.
